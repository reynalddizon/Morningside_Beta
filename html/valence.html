Content Types

follows

Twitter accounts most characteristically followed by this cluster, i.e. whom they follow much more frequently than members of other clusters do; "count" is the number of followers in the whole map.

mentions

Twitter accounts most characteristically mentioned by this cluster, i.e. whom they mention much more frequently than members of other clusters do; "count" is the number of other map members who've mentioned the indicated account.

retweets

Twitter accounts most characteristically retweeted by this cluster, i.e. whom they retweet much more frequently than members of other clusters do; "count" is the number of other map members who've retweeted the indicated account.

reply

Twitter accounts most characteristically replied to directly by this cluster, i.e. whom they reply directly to much more frequently than members of other clusters do; "count" is the number of other map members who've directly replied to the indicated account.

link_url

Websites preferred by members of this cluster, which they post much more frequently than others do; these links could be media sources, blogs, etc.; "count" is the number of other map members which have transmitted the URL.

hashtag

Hashtags preferred by members of this cluster, which they post much more frequently than others do; "count" is the number of other map members who have used the hashtag.

words

Single words most preferred in posts by this cluster; "count" is the number of map members who have used the word.

bi_pmi

Word pair collocations (pairs with special semantic significance, calculated using a pair-wise mutual information algorithm) most characteristically used in postings by this cluster; "count" is the number of other map members who have used the word pair.

bi_ihr

Word pair collocations (pairs with special semantic significance, calculated using a maximum likelihood ratio algorithm) most characteristically used in postings by this cluster; "count" is the number of other map members who have used the word pair.

tri_pmi

Word triplet collocations (pairs with special semantic significance, calculated using a pair-wise mutual information algorithm) most characteristically used in postings by this cluster; "count" is the number of other map members who have used the word triplet.
